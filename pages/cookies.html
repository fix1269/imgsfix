🍪 Cookies

Cookie Policy

The good news: imgsfix does not use tracking cookies. Here is exactly what we do and do not store on your device.

Home/ Cookies

Last updated: July 2026

The short version: imgsfix does not set tracking cookies of any kind. The only local storage we use is two small entries in your browser's localStorage to remember your theme (light/dark) and language (English/Arabic). This storage never leaves your device and contains no personal information. You can clear it at any time.

1. No Tracking Cookies

imgsfix does not use cookies to track you. We do not set first-party cookies for analytics, advertising, session management, personalization, or any other purpose. We do not place third-party cookies on your device. There is no cookie banner or consent popup on imgsfix because there are no tracking cookies to consent to.

Many websites use cookies to remember who you are, measure your behavior, serve targeted ads, or follow you across the web. imgsfix does none of this. We have no analytics, no advertising networks, no social media integrations, and no remarketing pixels — and therefore no need for the cookies that power them.

2. localStorage for Preferences Only

Instead of cookies, imgsfix uses the browser's localStorage API to remember two small preferences. localStorage is a browser feature that lets a website store small amounts of data on your device. Unlike cookies, localStorage data is not automatically sent to a server with every request — it stays on your device and is only read by the page that created it.

The two items we store are:

  • imgsfix_theme — a single word, either "light" or "dark", recording which visual theme you have selected. This lets the site load in your preferred theme on your next visit.
  • imgsfix_lang — a single word, either "en" or "ar", recording whether you have chosen English or Arabic. This lets the site display in your preferred language automatically.

That is the complete list. These values contain no personal information — they are short strings that describe a visual preference. They are never transmitted to us or to any third party. They exist solely to make your experience more convenient, and you can delete them at any time without affecting the functionality of any tool.

3. Google Fonts

imgsfix loads the Cairo typeface from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com). When your browser requests the font stylesheet and font files, Google's servers may set cookies on your device in accordance with Google's own privacy practices. These cookies are controlled by Google, not by imgsfix, and we have no access to them.

If you prefer not to receive any cookies from Google, you can:

  • Block third-party fonts in your browser settings or with a content blocker extension.
  • Use a browser extension that blocks requests to Google Fonts domains.
  • Configure your browser to block third-party cookies entirely.

If Google Fonts is blocked, imgsfix will fall back to your system's default fonts. All tools and features will continue to work normally — only the typography will change.

For more about Google's data practices, please refer to Google's Privacy Policy.

4. How to View and Clear localStorage

Because your preferences are stored locally on your device, you have full control over them. Here is how to view and clear imgsfix's localStorage in popular browsers:

Google Chrome / Microsoft Edge

  • Open imgsfix, then press F12 or Ctrl+Shift+I (Cmd+Option+I on Mac) to open Developer Tools.
  • Go to the Application tab.
  • In the left sidebar, expand Local Storage and select the imgsfix site.
  • You will see imgsfix_theme and imgsfix_lang. Right-click and choose Clear to remove them.

Mozilla Firefox

  • Open imgsfix, then press F12 or Ctrl+Shift+I (Cmd+Option+I on Mac) to open Developer Tools.
  • Go to the Storage tab.
  • Expand Local Storage and select the imgsfix site.
  • You will see the stored entries. Right-click and choose Delete to remove them.

Apple Safari

  • Open imgsfix, then press Cmd+Option+I to open Web Inspector.
  • Go to the Storage tab.
  • Expand Local Storage and select the imgsfix site.
  • Select the entries and press Delete to remove them.

Clearing All Site Data

You can also clear all localStorage for every site at once through your browser's privacy settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Clear browsing data → Cached images and files / Cookies and site data.
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Clear Data.
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data → Remove All.
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies and site data.

5. Your Control Over Preferences

You are always in control of your imgsfix experience. Your theme and language preferences are optional conveniences — the tools work perfectly without them. If you clear localStorage, the site will simply use its defaults (light theme and your browser's detected language) until you choose again.

There is no account to delete, no data to export, and no tracking history to review, because none of those things exist. Your relationship with imgsfix is as simple as it gets: you use the tools, your images stay on your device, and the only thing the site remembers is how you like it to look.

6. Changes to This Policy

If we ever change our cookie or storage practices, we will update this page and revise the "last updated" date. Given that imgsfix's core design avoids cookies entirely, we do not anticipate changes that would introduce tracking. Any future change would be clearly documented here.
